## 📜 About This Translation

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights, adopted by the United Nations General Assembly on December 10, 1948, is one of the most important documents in human history. It establishes fundamental rights and freedoms that belong to every person, everywhere.

This translation demonstrates the Universal Ontological Language's capability to express complex ethical, legal, and philosophical concepts with clarity and precision.

## 📖 Preamble / Prologue

### **Original (English)**
> Whereas recognition of the inherent dignity and of the equal and inalienable rights of all members of the human family is the foundation of freedom, justice and peace in the world...

### **Translation**
```
ǰe epo nu ařu la oho nu iho va ri-xa-i,
because people all identity and rights all equal is state-real,

la iho nu va av-iha,

de udu va uda ňu fa-xa, li-xa, la sa-ri-i pi umu
the structure is foundation of freedom, justice, and peace-state at world

...
```

## Article 1 / Artigo 1

### **Original (English)**
> All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights. They are endowed with reason and conscience and should act towards one another in a spirit of brotherhood.

### **Translation**
```
epo nu ta ve-ipi pi fa-xa la fa-oři
people all past become-life at equal-real and equal-quality

pi ařu la oho.
at identity and rights.

bu vi ečo la či-ali,
they have reason and conscience-good,

la mo ve pa eke nu pi uv-ewo ňu eki.
and must do toward sibling all in spirit-love of family.
```

### **Literal Translation**
"All people were born-into-life at equal-reality and equal-quality at identity and rights. They have reason and conscience-good, and must do toward all siblings in spirit-love of family."

## Article 4 / Artigo 4

### **Original (English)**
> No one shall be held in slavery or servitude; slavery and the slave trade shall be prohibited in all their forms.

### **Translation**
```
epa na va pi ovi-vu-epu,
person none is at work-forced-person (slavery),

le pi ovi-re-epu.
or at work-passive-person (servitude).

ovi-vu-epu la uhi-epu-ovi va em nu pi adi nu.
work-forced-person and exchange-person-work is forbidden all at form all.
```

**Literal:**
"No person is at forced-work-person (slavery) or passive-work-person (servitude). Forced-work-person and person-work-exchange is forbidden in all forms."

## 📝 Translation Notes

### **Challenges & Solutions**

**1. Abstract Legal Concepts**

Original concept: "inalienable"  
Solution: `aviha` = not-separable (av = not + iha = separable)

**2. Cultural Terms**

Original: "brotherhood"  
Solution: `uviewo ňu eki` = spirit-love of family  
(More universal than gender-specific "brotherhood")

**3. Complex Rights Terminology**

Original: "entitled to"  
Solution: `vi oho pa` = has rights to  
(Simple construction from core vocabulary)

**4. Building New Concepts**

When core vocabulary insufficient:
- **Torture:** `opoiho` = body-harm
- **Slavery:** `ovivu-epu` = work-forced-person
- **Cruel:** `veavu-epo` = action-rage-extreme-people

**Principle:** Build from existing words rather than creating arbitrary new terms.
