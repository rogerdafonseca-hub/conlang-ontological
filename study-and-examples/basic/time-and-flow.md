# ⏳✨ Time & Flow — A Beginner’s Guide to Tense (T) and Aspect (G)

This guide explains how to express **time** and **event structure** using two core families:

- **T (Tense)** → *When something happens*
- **G (Aspect)** → *How the action unfolds in time*

Together, they form a powerful and intuitive system.

---

# 🧭 1. FAMILY T — Tense (When?)

Tense places an action along a timeline using a natural scale:

> **a → e → i → o → u = past → present → future**

### Basic Tense Particles

| Particle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **ta** | past |
| **te** | near past |
| **ti** | present |
| **to** | near future |
| **tu** | future |

### Examples

- **ta va** → happened  
- **ti va** → happens / is happening  
- **tu va** → will happen  

---

# 🔁 2. FAMILY G — Aspect (How?)

Aspect describes the *shape* of the action in time — whether it happens once, repeats, continues, or finishes.

### Basic Aspect Scale

| Particle | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **ga** | punctual (once) |
| **ge** | repeated / habitual |
| **gi** | ongoing |
| **go** | continuous |
| **gu** | complete |

---

# ⚙️ 3. Combining T + G

The structure is:

> **T + G + Verb**

---

# 🧪 4. Practical Examples

### 📍 Single event in the past
- **ta ga va** → happened once  

### 🔁 Repeated action in the past
- **ta ge va** → used to happen  

### ⏳ Action happening now
- **ti gi va** → is happening  

### 🌊 Continuous action
- **ti go va** → is continuously happening  

### ✅ Completed action
- **ta gu va** → has fully happened  

### 🔮 Future event
- **tu ga va** → will happen  

---

# 🧠 Key Idea

- **T (Tense)** = position in time  
- **G (Aspect)** = structure of the action  

Together:

> You can describe *when* and *how* something happens with just two particles.

---

# 🚀 Final Insight

By combining **T + G**, you can express:

- time (past, present, future)
- repetition
- duration
- completion

All with a simple and consistent system based on vowel gradation.
