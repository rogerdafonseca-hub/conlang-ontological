## 🎯 What Is This?

A **universal constructed language (conlang)** built on rigorous ontological foundations, designed to express any human concept with precision while remaining accessible to learners worldwide.

**Three core goals:**
- ✅ **Complete** — express anything from quantum physics to poetry
- ✅ **Lightweight** — 30 phonemes, regular grammar, learnable patterns
- ✅ **Intuitive** — based on cross-linguistic universals, not cultural bias

Unlike natural languages shaped by historical accidents, this system is **systematically constructed** from first principles:
- Universal phonetic patterns (IPA Top 25 consonants)
- Cross-linguistic grammatical universals
- Formal ontology (25 fundamental domains)
- International vocabulary (cognates from 5+ language families)

## 🌟 Why This Matters

Most constructed languages optimize for some goals while sacrificing others:

| Language | Complete | Lightweight | Intuitive | Issues |

| **Esperanto** | ⚠️ Limited | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Euro-centric, lacks precision |

| **Toki Pona** | ❌ No | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Only ~120 words, can't express complexity |
| **This Language** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | Balances all three |

## ⚡ Key Features

### 🔤 Universal Phonology
- **25 consonants** from IPA Top 25 (present in 50-95% of languages)
- **5 vowels** (a, e, i, o, u) — universal triangle
- **30 phonemes total** — comparable to Spanish or Japanese

### 📐 4-Layer Architecture
Progressive complexity — learn what you need:

1. **Basic Grammar (CV)** — 125 essential particles
2. **Extended Grammar (VCV)** — 525 nuanced resources  
3. **Nominal Lexicon (CV+CV+)** — unlimited borrowed/native words
4. **Ontological Vectors (VCV+CV+)** — 390,625 precise concepts

### 🧬 Dual-Tier System

**Essential Core (78,125 concepts)** — For everyone
- Diagonal vowel pattern: `apa, epe, ipi, opo, upu`
- Covers 95% of daily communication
- Learn in 2-5 years

**Professional Expansion (390,625 concepts)** — For specialists
- Full vowel matrix (all 25 combinations)
- Scientific precision, AI systems, ontological research
- Optional — use only when needed

**You choose your depth.**

### 🌐 International Vocabulary
Common words borrowed from cross-linguistic cognates:
- `kafe` — coffee (English), café (Spanish/French/Portuguese), caffè (Italian), 咖啡 kāfēi (Mandarin)
- `telefone` — telephone/teléfono/téléphone/telefono
- `pitisa` — pizza (universal in 100+ languages)

## 🏗️ System Architecture

### Layer 1: Basic Grammar (CV) — 125 Resources

**Structure:** Consonant + Vowel

25 grammatical families × 5 vowels = **125 fundamental particles**

**Examples:**
- **k-family (interrogatives):** `ka` (what?), `ke` (who?), `ki` (where?), `ko` (when?), `ku` (how/why?)
- **m-family (modality):** `ma` (can), `me` (must), `mi` (may), `mo` (should), `mu` (able to)
- **t-family (time):** `ta` (past), `te` (present), `ti` (future), `to` (perfect), `tu` (progressive)

[See full grammar →](docs/03-grammar-cv.md)

---

### Layer 2: Extended Grammar (VCV) — 525 Resources

**Structure:** Vowel + Consonant + Vowel

Systematic vowel gradation expands each family to 21 clusters:

| Gradation | Meaning | Example (modality) |
|-----------|---------|-------------------|
| **a-** | Minimal/negative | `ama` (cannot) |
| **e-** | Weak/beginning | `ema` (maybe can) |
| **i-** | Neutral/standard | `ima` (can, neutral) |
| **o-** | Strong/intense | `oma` (very likely can) |
| **u-** | Maximal/absolute | `uma` (certainly can) |

**Pattern repeats across all 25 families.**

[See full system →](docs/04-grammar-vcv.md)

---

### Layer 3: Nominal Lexicon (CV+CV+) — Unlimited

**Structure:** Multiple CV syllables (consonant-initial)

**Two strategies:**
1. **International borrowings:** Adapt cognates from 5+ languages
2. **Native constructions:** Systematic CV+CV combinations

**Borrowing criteria:**
- Present in 5+ languages from 3+ families
- 70%+ phonetic similarity
- Same meaning in all sources
- Adaptable to CV+CV+ structure

[See lexicon framework →](docs/06-lexicon.md)

---

### Layer 4: Ontological Vectors (VCV+CV+) — 390,625 Concepts

**Structure:** VCV (domain) + CV (descriptors)...

**25 ontological domains × comprehensive structure:**

| # | Domain | Questions Answered |
|---|--------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Substance/Being** (p) | What is it? What type? |
| 2 | **Dimensional/Spatial** (m) | Where? What shape? |
| 3 | **Gradual/Temporal** (n) | When? How long? |
| 4 | **Dynamic/Processual** (t) | What process? |
| 5 | **Social/Collective** (k) | What group? |
| ... | *[20 more domains]* | ... |

**Each domain contains:**
- 5 subcategories (different aspects)
- 25 vectors per subcategory (specific variations)
- 5 gradations per vector (intensity a→u)

**Total:** 25 domains × 5 subcategories × 25 vectors × 5 gradations × 5 variations = **390,625 concepts**

[See vector system →](docs/05-vector-system.md)

You can communicate effectively at any level:

**Beginner (Months 1-3)**
- Basic grammar (125 CV particles)
- Essential matrices (diagonal vowels only)
- 500 borrowed words
- **Result:** Simple conversations, basic needs

**Intermediate (Year 1)**
- Extended grammar (525 VCV particles)
- Essential Core complete (78,125 concepts)
- 2,000 word vocabulary
- **Result:** Read literature, write clearly, discuss complex topics

**Advanced (Years 2-5)**
- Professional Expansion (390,625 concepts)

- **Result:** Technical precision, scientific writing, AI integration
