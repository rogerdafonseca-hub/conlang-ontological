# Field PAPA — Matter (Core)

**Domain:** P — Substance / Brute Matter  
**Field:** PP — Pure nucleus (same-domain pair)  
**Matrix position:** PA- × -PA (most general field of the most general domain)  
**Ontological type:** Universal material categories — what things are made of  
**Lexical layer:** Léxico Nominal — CVCV roots

---

## Field Definition

PAPA covers the most universal and cognitively fundamental categories of brute matter. These are the substances that every human culture perceives, names, and interacts with directly — independent of function, use, or technical classification.

**Inclusion criterion:** A concept belongs to PAPA if it answers "what is this thing made of?" without referencing function (D), process (V), form (L), or living organisms (N).

**Exclusion criterion:** Concepts that are primarily processes (fire, combustion), artifacts (tools, containers), or organisms are excluded regardless of their material nature.

## C₂ Tendency Map

The second consonant carries a soft semantic signal within domain P:

| C₂ | Tendency | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| r | fluid / continuous | water, oil, blood, sap |
| t | solid / rigid | stone, bone, salt, ice |
| k | granular / particulate | metal, sand, coal, grain |
| m | malleable / plastic | clay, gel, fat, wax |
| s | diffuse / gaseous | air, vapor, dust, ash |
| n | organic / biological | soil, skin, poison |
| l | structured / formed | wood, glass, ceramic, fiber |

This is a **preferential rule, not obligatory**. Exceptions are permitted when phonological clarity or collision avoidance requires it (see: *poga* — gum).

## Ontological Position

In the **sistema ontológico**, PAPA maps to the most general field of domain P:

```
VCV(P) + PA + PA = domain P, field PA, area PA
```

This is the ontological address of all 36 words in this field. More specific material concepts (gold, copper, quartz) belong to deeper fields: PAPE, PAPI, and beyond.

## Notes on Exceptions

**poga** (gum): C₂=g breaks the malleable/m tendency. Kept because the m-cluster (pame, peme, pomi, pomu, pama) was already saturated and phonological clarity outweighs structural purity. This is an explicit, controlled exception.

**pixa** (acid): C₂=x used for corrosive matter. No existing tendency covered this semantic territory cleanly. Explicit exception.

> Principle: **perceptual clarity > structural purity**, when the exception is conscious and documented.
