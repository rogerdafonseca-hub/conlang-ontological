## 🏛️ The Big Picture

### **Four Distinct Systems, One Coherent Language**

The Universal Ontological Language consists of four interconnected but visually distinct systems:

**System 1: Grammar (CV + VC)** — 250 particles  
Provides structural framework. Fixed forever.

**System 2: Core Vocabulary (VCV)** — 625 words  
Provides everyday communication. Systematically derived.

**System 3: Nominal Lexicon (CV+CV+...)** — Unlimited  
Names specific objects, creates compounds, handles all content words.

**System 4: Technical Vectors (VCV+CV+...)** — 390,625 concepts  
Provides precision, formality, and specificity. Optional depth.

**For fluency:** Systems 1 + 2 = **875 resources**  
**For everything:** All four systems working together

## 📊 System 1: Grammar (CV + VC)

### **Purpose:** Provide structural framework for all sentences

### **CV System — 125 Basic Particles**

**Pattern:** Consonant + Vowel (single syllable, starts with consonant)

**Structure:** 25 families × 5 particles each

**Examples:**

**Family B (Pronouns):**  
ba = I, me  
be = you  
bi = they (singular)  
bo = we  
bu = they (plural)

**Family M (Modal):**  
ma = can  
me = may  
mi = neutral/indicative  
mo = must  
mu = will

**Family T (Tense):**  
ta = past  
te = present  
ti = timeless  
to = future  
tu = immediate

**Total:** 25 families × 5 = **125 particles**

---

### **VC System — 125 Extended Particles**

**Pattern:** Vowel + Consonant (single syllable, starts with vowel)

**Structure:** Mirrors CV families with extensions/variations

**Examples:**

**Family B (Reflexive/Reciprocal):**  
ab = myself  
eb = yourself  
ib = themselves  
ob = ourselves  
ub = each other

**Family M (Modal Extensions):**  
am = cannot  
em = may not  
im = might  
om = should  
um = certainly

**Family T (Aspect):**  
at = past habitual  
et = present continuous  
it = present perfect  
ot = future intentional  
ut = past perfect

**Total:** 25 families × 5 = **125 particles**

---

### **Grammar System Total: 250 particles**

**Recognition rule:**  
- Starts with **consonant** + 1 syllable = **CV** (basic grammar)  
- Starts with **vowel** + 1 syllable + ends in consonant = **VC** (extended grammar)

**Function:** These never change. Fixed forever. Memorize once, use forever.

**CRITICAL PRONUNCIATION NOTE:**

Grammar particles must be pronounced with slight pauses between them to avoid fusion:

✅ Correct:  
`ba ma ivi` (I can do) — pronounced: ba' ma' ivi  
`be ta ma ivi` (you past can do) — pronounced: be' ta' ma' ivi

❌ Incorrect:  
`bama` (sounds like you're trying to fuse ba + ma)  
`betamaivi` (sounds like one word instead of four)

**Grammar particles ONLY combine with other systems, never with each other in fusion.**

When speaking, maintain clear syllable boundaries: **be' ta' ma' ivi** NOT **betamaivi**

## 📊 System 2: Core Vocabulary (VCV)

### **Purpose:** Provide everyday words for daily communication

### **VCV System — 625 Essential Words**

**Pattern:** Vowel + Consonant + Vowel (single syllable, starts with vowel, ends with vowel)

**Structure:** 25 domains × 25 words each (5 concepts × 5 intensities)

**Domain V (Action):**  
ava, ave, avi, avo, avu = Rest (minimal to maximal)  
eva, eve, evi, evo, evu = Begin (minimal to maximal)  
iva, ive, ivi, ivo, ivu = Do/Make (minimal to maximal)  
ova, ove, ovi, ovo, ovu = Work (minimal to maximal)  
uva, uve, uvi, uvo, uvu = Force/Push (minimal to maximal)

**Domain W (Emotion):**  
awa, awe, awi, awo, awu = Anger expression (frown to rage)  
ewa, ewe, ewi, ewo, ewu = Sadness expression (sigh to wail)  
iwa, iwe, iwi, iwo, iwu = Indifference (overlook to despise)  
owa, owe, owi, owo, owu = Surprise (notice to marvel)  
uwa, uwe, uwi, uwo, uwu = Happiness (smile to exult)

**Domain M (Space):**  
ama, ame, ami, amo, amu = Location/Place  
ema, eme, emi, emo, emu = Proximity (near to far)  
ima, ime, imi, imo, imu = Direction/Path  
oma, ome, omi, omo, omu = Container/Space  
uma, ume, umi, umo, umu = Landmark/Center

**Total:** 25 domains × 25 words = **625 words**

---

### **Domain Structure:**

**Each domain has:**  
- 5 core concepts (represented by first vowel: a/e/i/o/u)  
- 5 intensity levels (represented by last vowel: a/e/i/o/u)  
- = 25 words per domain

**Intensity scale (universal across all domains):**  
**-a** = minimal, negative, absent  
**-e** = weak, beginning, emerging  
**-i** = neutral, standard, typical  
**-o** = strong, intense, advanced  
**-u** = maximal, absolute, extreme

---

### **Recognition rule:**

Starts with **vowel** + 1 syllable + ends in **vowel** = **VCV** (core vocabulary)

**Function:** Master these 625 words → achieve everyday fluency

## 📊 System 3: Nominal Lexicon (CV+CV+...)

### **Purpose:** Name specific objects, create compounds, handle numerals

### **Nominal Words — Unlimited**

**Pattern:** Consonant + Vowel + Consonant + Vowel + ... (multiple syllables, starts with consonant)

**Structure:** Compositional and expandable

**Simple Objects (to be officially defined):**  
Words for common concrete objects like animals, plants, colors, tools, etc.

**Compounds:**  
Combine multiple CV syllables to create specific compound concepts.

**Letter N is NOT reserved exclusively for numerals** — N can be used freely in the nominal lexicon for any words.

**Total:** Unlimited (created as needed)

---

### **Numeral System — Universal Z-Marker**

**IMPORTANT:** Numerals use a special marking system that prevents conflicts with grammar and frees all consonants for general use in the nominal lexicon.

**All numerals begin with Z + vowel marker:**

**Numeral Types:**

**za-** = Cardinal numbers (one, two, three...)  
**ze-** = Ordinal numbers (first, second, third...)  
**zi-** = Fractional numbers (half, third, quarter...)  
**zo-** = Multiplicative numbers (double, triple...)  
**zu-** = Collective numbers (pair, trio, quartet...)

---

### **Digit Structure:**

**Base digits use N and S consonants:**

**0-4:** na, ne, ni, no, nu  
**5-9:** sa, se, si, so, su

**Positional structure:**  
1 syllable = units (0-9)  
2 syllables = tens (10-99)  
3 syllables = hundreds (100-999)  
4 syllables = thousands (1000+)

---

### **Cardinal Numbers (za-):**

**Single digits:**  
zana = 0  
zane = 1  
zani = 2  
zano = 3  
zanu = 4  
zasa = 5  
zase = 6  
zasi = 7  
zaso = 8

**Compound numbers:**  
zanena = 10 (za + ne-na = 1-0)

zanenu = 14 (za + ne-nu = 1-4)  
zanenunosi = 1437 (za + ne-nu-no-si = 1-4-3-7)  
zasasusi = 569 (za + sa-su-si = 5-6-9)

---

### **Ordinal Numbers (ze-):**

zene = 1st (first)  
zeni = 2nd (second)  
zeno = 3rd (third)  
zenenu = 14th (fourteenth)  
zenesasa = 155th (one hundred fifty-fifth)

---

### **Fractional Numbers (zi-):**

zini = 1/2 (half)

---

### **Multiplicative Numbers (zo-):**

---

### **Collective Numbers (zu-):**

---

### **Recognition rule:**

Starts with **Z + vowel** = **Numeral** (any type)  
Starts with **consonant (not Z)** + 2+ syllables = **Nominal lexicon** (objects/compounds)

**Function:** Z-marker provides universal numeral system while keeping nominal lexicon completely free

## 🎨 Word Formation Rules

### **1. Grammar Particles (CV/VC)**

**Rule:** Fixed. Never modified. Never combined with each other in fusion.

✅ Correct:  
ba ma ivi (I can do) — Pronounce: ba' ma' ivi  
be ta ma ivi (you past can do = you could do) — Pronounce: be' ta' ma' ivi

❌ Incorrect:  
bama (trying to combine ba + ma into one sound)  
mataivi (trying to fuse grammar particles)

**CRITICAL:** Maintain clear pauses between grammar particles when speaking.  
**be' ta' ma' ivi** NOT **betamaivi**

**Grammar particles ONLY combine with other systems, never with each other in fusion.**

---

### **2. Vocabulary Words (VCV)**

**Rule:** Used as-is. Can be modified by grammar. Cannot be combined into compounds.

✅ Correct:  
ba ivi (I do)  
ba ta ivi (I past do = I did)  
ba ma ivi (I can do)

❌ Incorrect:  
iviavi (trying to compound VCV words together)

**VCV words are atomic. Use them individually.**

---

### **3. Nominal Lexicon (CV+CV+)**

**Rule:** Freely combinable. Build compounds as needed. All consonants available (N is NOT reserved).

✅ Correct:  
Simple objects (to be defined)  
Compound objects (combine CV syllables)

**Nominal words can be combined infinitely to create new specific terms.**

---

### **4. Numerals (Z+V+digits)**

**Rule:** Always begin with Z + vowel marker. Follow positional structure.

✅ Correct:  
zane = 1 (cardinal)  
zeno = 3rd (ordinal)  
zanenu = 14 (cardinal)  
zenesasa = 155th (ordinal)

❌ Incorrect:  
ne = trying to use bare digit (conflicts with grammar)  
nenu = trying to use N-pattern without Z-marker

**All numerals MUST use Z-marker system.**

---

### **5. Technical Vectors (VCV+CV+)**

**Rule:** Written as one word. Built from vector components. Used for precision, formality, and depth.

✅ Correct:  
apimabi (written together)  
→ biological organism

❌ Incorrect:  
api-ma-bi (separated with hyphens in actual use — only use hyphens for teaching/analysis)

**Vectors are written together as single words in actual usage.**

## 🔍 Disambiguation Strategies

### **When Patterns Conflict:**

**Numeral System (Z-marker) Eliminates Most Conflicts:**

The Z-marker system for numerals eliminates the historical conflict between grammar particles and number words. Letter N is now freely usable in the nominal lexicon.

**For rare vocabulary conflicts:**

**Problem:** Grammar VC ends the same as Vocabulary VCV beginning

**Example:**  
Grammar: ař (mine — possessive pronoun)  
Vocabulary: aře (mark, label)

**Solution 1: Prolongation (writing)**

Grammar: ař (written as-is)  
Vocabulary: ařaa (double final vowel)

**Solution 2: Tone (speech)**

Grammar: ař [neutral tone]  
Vocabulary: aře [rising tone ↗]

**Solution 3: Context**

ba ař → "my/mine" (possessive clearly)  
ba ařaa → "mark/label" (noun clearly)

**This disambiguation applies to approximately 5 patterns per conflicting domain.**  
Most words never need special handling.

## 📐 Sentence Structure

### **Basic Word Order:**

**SVO (Subject-Verb-Object) — Like English**

ba ivi api  
I do thing  
→ "I do the thing"

bi evi ami  
they take place  
→ "They go to the place"

---

### **Flexibility through Particles:**

Because grammar particles mark relationships, word order can flex for emphasis:

**Standard (SVO):**  
ba ivi api pi ami  
I do thing at place  
→ "I do the thing at the place"

**Emphasis on location (fronted):**  
pi ami ba ivi api  
at place I do thing  
→ "At the place, I do the thing"

**Emphasis on object (fronted):**  
api ba ivi pi ami  
thing I do at place  
→ "The thing, I do at the place"

**Particles maintain clarity even when order changes.**
