## 🎯 Introduction

The **Lexicon** contains common, everyday words borrowed from international sources and adapted to the language's phonology.

### **Two Vocabulary Systems**

This language has **two parallel systems** for creating words:

|System|Structure|Use Case|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Lexicon (CV+CV+)**|Borrowed/native words, start with **C**|Common everyday concepts|`kafe` (coffee), `pitisa` (pizza)|
|**Vectors (VCV+CV+)**|Compositional ontological, start with **V**|Precise technical concepts|`apa-ma-ri` (systematic description)|

**Both are valid.** Use whichever fits the context.

---

### **Why Borrow Words?**

**Advantages:**

- ✅ **Instant recognition** — learners already know many words
- ✅ **Easier learning** — less memorization required
- ✅ **Cross-cultural fairness** — words from many language families
- ✅ **Practical efficiency** — saying "kafe" is faster than building a vector

**Philosophy:**

> "Don't reinvent the wheel. If humanity already has a near-universal word for coffee, use it."

---

## 🌍 Borrowing Philosophy

### **Core Principles**

**1. Universal Recognition**

- Words must be recognizable across **multiple language families**
- Not just Indo-European — include Asian, African, Semitic sources

**2. Phonological Integration**

- All borrowed words adapt to **CV+CV+** structure
- No complex clusters, codas, or non-native sounds

**3. Semantic Stability**

- Words must mean the **same thing** in all source languages
- Avoid false friends (words that look similar but differ in meaning)

**4. Frequency Priority**

- Borrow the **most common** concepts first
- High-frequency words get priority

**5. Systematic Expansion**

- Related words follow predictable patterns
- Build word families (e.g., kafe → kafeteria)

---

## ✅ Borrowing Criteria

### **Strict Requirements**

For a word to be borrowed into the lexicon:

#### **Criterion 1: Cross-Linguistic Presence**

**Requirement:** Present in **5+ languages** from **3+ different language families**

**Language families considered:**

- Indo-European (English, Spanish, Russian, Hindi, etc.)
- Sino-Tibetan (Mandarin, Cantonese, etc.)
- Afro-Asiatic (Arabic, Hebrew, Amharic, etc.)
- Niger-Congo (Swahili, Yoruba, etc.)
- Austronesian (Indonesian, Tagalog, Malay, etc.)
- Japonic (Japanese)
- Turkic (Turkish, Uzbek, etc.)
- Others as relevant

---

#### **Criterion 2: Phonetic Similarity**

**Requirement:** At least **70% phonetic overlap** across source languages

**Method:** Compare IPA transcriptions, count matching segments

**Example — "coffee":**

|Language|Word|IPA|Similarity|
|---|---|---|---|
|English|coffee|/ˈkɒfi/|✅|
|Spanish|café|/kaˈfe/|✅|
|Portuguese|café|/kaˈfɛ/|✅|
|French|café|/kafe/|✅|
|Arabic|قهوة|/qahwa/|⚠️ Different|
|Mandarin|咖啡|/kāfēi/|✅|
|Japanese|コーヒー|/koːhiː/|✅|

**Analysis:** 6/7 have /k-f/ core → **qualifies** (adapt as `kafe`)

---

#### **Criterion 3: Semantic Consistency**

**Requirement:** Same meaning in **all** source languages

**Disqualified example — "gift":**

|Language|Word|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|English|gift|present (noun) ✅|
|German|Gift|poison ❌|

**Result:** False friend → **disqualified**

---

#### **Criterion 4: Cultural Neutrality**

**Requirement:** No strong cultural/religious associations that limit universal use

**Examples:**

- ✅ "pizza" — food item, neutral
- ✅ "radio" — technology, neutral
- ❌ "baptism" — Christian-specific term
- ❌ "karma" — Hindu/Buddhist-specific

**Note:** Religious/cultural terms can enter as **descriptive vectors** instead

## 🔧 Adaptation Rules

### **Phonological Adaptation Process**

All borrowed words must conform to **CV+CV+** syllable structure.

---

### **Rule 1: Simplify Consonant Clusters**

**Method:** Insert vowels or reduce clusters

|Original|Cluster|Adapted|Method|
|---|---|---|---|
|sport|/sp-/|**sopore** or **seporo**|Insert vowel|
|text|/kst/|**tekise**|Insert vowels|
|street|/str-/|**setirete**|Insert vowels|

**Choose vowel based on:**

- Phonetic environment
- Ease of pronunciation
- Avoiding homophone conflicts

---

### **Rule 2: Eliminate Codas**

**Method:** Add final vowel

|Original|Ends with|Adapted|Method|
|---|---|---|---|
|internet|-t|**internete**|Add -e|
|bus|-s|**basu** or **buse**|Add vowel|
|film|-m|**filme**|Add -e|

**Default final vowels:**

- `-e` for most words
- `-i` for words ending in /i/-like sounds
- `-o` for words ending in /o/-like sounds
- `-a`, `-u` as needed

---

### **Rule 3: Substitute Non-Native Sounds**

**Replace sounds NOT in the 25-consonant inventory:**

|Non-native|Substitute|Example|
|---|---|---|
|/θ/ (th-think)|/t/ or /s/|"theater" → **teatro**|
|/ð/ (th-this)|/d/|"this" → **disi**|
|/ŋ/ word-initial|/n/|rare in borrowing|

**Most international cognates already use native sounds.**

---

### **Rule 4: Preserve Recognizability**

**Priority:** Keep the word **recognizable** to speakers of source languages

**Balance:**

- Adapt enough to fit phonology
- Don't adapt so much it becomes unrecognizable

**Example — "telephone":**

- ❌ Too much change: "tapano" (unrecognizable)
- ✅ Balanced: "telefone" (recognizable + compliant)

---

### **Rule 5: Avoid Homophones**

**Check:** Does the adapted form conflict with existing words?

**If yes:**

- Adjust vowels slightly
- Add syllable for distinction
- Use vector instead

**Example:**

- If `kasa` already means X
- Don't adapt "casa" (house) as `kasa`
- Use `kasaha` or similar variant

## 🆕 How to Propose New Words

### **Contribution Process**

Want to suggest a word for the lexicon? Follow these steps:

---

#### **Step 1: Check Eligibility**

**Ask yourself:**

1. Is this concept common/frequent? (Top 2,000 most-used words?)
2. Is there an international cognate? (5+ languages, 3+ families?)
3. Does it have the same meaning everywhere?
4. Is it culturally neutral?

**If YES to all:** Continue to Step 2  
**If NO to any:** Consider if a **vector** would be better

---

#### **Step 2: Research the Word**

**Gather data:**

1. Find the word in 10 reference languages (see Source Languages)
2. Transcribe each into IPA
3. Note meanings in each language
4. Check for false friends

**Use resources:**

- Wiktionary (etymologies)
- Google Translate (quick checks)
- Native speaker input (best!)

---

#### **Step 3: Calculate Similarity**

**Method:**

Count matching phonetic features across languages:

- Initial consonant matches?
- Vowel pattern matches?
- Final sound matches?

**Example — "radio":**

|Language|Form|IPA|Match Score|
|---|---|---|---|
|English|radio|/ˈreɪdioʊ/|Base|
|Spanish|radio|/ˈradjo/|80%|
|Portuguese|rádio|/ˈʁadʒu/|70%|
|French|radio|/ʁadjo/|80%|
|Mandarin|收音机|/shōuyīnjī/|0% ❌|
|Japanese|ラジオ|/radjio/|90%|
|Arabic|راديو|/raːdju/|75%|
|Russian|радио|/ˈradʲɪo/|80%|

**Analysis:** 7/8 have strong /rad-/ core → **qualifies**

**Threshold:** ≥70% similarity in ≥5 languages from ≥3 families

---

#### **Step 4: Adapt to Phonology**

Apply adaptation rules:

1. Simplify clusters → insert vowels
2. Remove codas → add final vowel
3. Substitute non-native sounds
4. Choose CV+CV+ structure

**Example — "radio":**

- Original: /ˈreɪdioʊ/
- Simplified: /radio/
- Check: ra-di-o (CV-CV-V) ✅
- **Adapted form:** `radio`

---

#### **Step 5: Check for Conflicts**

**Search existing lexicon:**

- Does `radio` already mean something else?
- Does it sound too similar to another word?

**If conflict:**

- Adjust vowels: `radyo`, `radiu`
- Add syllable: `radiofe`
- Use alternative: vector or different cognate

---

#### **Step 6: Submit Proposal**

**Open a GitHub Issue** with this template:

```markdown
## Lexicon Proposal: [word]

**Proposed form:** [adapted word]  
**IPA:** /[pronunciation]/  
**Meaning:** [definition]  
**Category:** [semantic field]

### Evidence of International Presence

| Language | Word | IPA | Meaning | Notes |
|----------|------|-----|---------|-------|
| English | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Spanish | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| Mandarin | ... | ... | ... | ... |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

**Similarity score:** [X]% across [N] languages

### Phonological Adaptation

**Original form:** [source]  
**Adaptation steps:**
1. [step 1]
2. [step 2]

**Final form:** [adapted word]

### Rationale

[Why this word should be included]

### Conflicts Checked

- [ ] No homophone conflicts
- [ ] No semantic conflicts
- [ ] Recognizable to source language speakers
```

---

#### **Step 7: Community Review**

**What happens next:**

1. Maintainers review proposal
2. Community discusses (via GitHub comments)
3. Native speakers verify recognizability
4. Phonologists check adaptation
5. Decision made (accept, revise, or reject)

**If accepted:**

- Word added to lexicon
- Documented in this file
- Added to dictionary JSON file (when created)

## 🎯 Design Goals

### **What We're Building**

**Target lexicon characteristics:**

✅ **Internationally recognized** — average speaker recognizes 60%+ of words  
✅ **Cross-linguistically fair** — no single language family dominates  
✅ **Phonologically integrated** — all words feel native to the system  
✅ **Semantically precise** — no ambiguity or false friends  
✅ **Expandable** — clear process for adding new words  
✅ **Documented** — etymology and rationale for every entry

---

### **What We're Avoiding**

❌ **Eurocentrism** — not just Latin/Greek roots  
❌ **Arbitrary creation** — no "made up" words when cognates exist  
❌ **Phonological chaos** — strict adherence to CV+CV+  
❌ **Cultural bias** — neutral terminology  
❌ **Closed system** — community can propose additions
