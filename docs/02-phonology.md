# 📜 PHONETICS & PRONUNCIATION — VERSION 0.8.0

## 🔹 Purpose

This document teaches **how to pronounce the language correctly**.

> Focus: **sound, articulation, and clarity**  
> Not: grammar, ontology, or structure

All sounds are defined using the **International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)**.

---

# 🔤 VOWELS

Vowels are **pure, stable, and always clearly pronounced**.  
They never reduce and never change quality.

|Letter|IPA|Description|Example (approx.)|
|---|---|---|---|
|**A**|/a/|open, central|“a” in _father_|
|**E**|/e/|mid-front|“e” in _they_ (no glide)|
|**I**|/i/|high-front|“ee” in _see_|
|**O**|/o/|mid-back|“o” in _go_ (no glide)|
|**U**|/u/|high-back|“oo” in _food_|

### 🔊 Examples

- **pa** → /pa/
    
- **pe** → /pe/
    
- **pi** → /pi/
    
- **po** → /po/
    
- **pu** → /pu/

# 🔊 CONSONANT CLUSTERS (EC)

Clusters may occur as part of the system, but:

> They are **not modified, expanded, or repaired artificially**

---

## 🔹 Natural Clusters (spoken as-is)

|Form|IPA|
|---|---|
|**kra**|/kɾa/|
|**pla**|/pla/|
|**bra**|/bɾa/|

---

## 🔹 Restricted Clusters

Some clusters are difficult or unstable:

- ml
    
- xr
    
- ľr
    
- similar combinations
    

### Rule:

> These are **not forced into speech**

Instead:

- They remain in **structural representation**
    
- Spoken forms emerge **naturally through derivation or context**

# 🔊 RHYTHM & STRESS

> Stress is **even and stable**

- No strong stress peaks
    
- Each syllable is clear
    

Examples:

- **akra** → /a.kɾa/
    
- **ekli** → /e.kli/
    
- **mili** → /mi.li/
    

---

# 🔊 PRONUNCIATION PRINCIPLE

> Do not force articulation.

If a form feels:

- natural → pronounce directly
    
- difficult → it is likely not meant to be spoken in that form
