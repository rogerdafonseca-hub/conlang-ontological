# 🔊 PHONOLOGY — Sound System

**Complete Phonological Inventory and Rules**

_Version 0.6.5 — Definitive Structure_

---

## 🎯 Overview

The phonological system is designed for **maximum cross-linguistic accessibility** while maintaining **systematic distinctiveness**. With only 35 phonemes (20 consonants + 10 clusters + 5 vowels), the language achieves remarkable productivity through combinatorial power rather than phonemic abundance.

**Design Principles:**
- Common sounds across world languages
- Clear acoustic distinctions
- No rare or difficult articulations
- Minimal phonotactic constraints
- Systematic cluster formation

## 🎵 Phonotactic Rules

### **Syllable Structure**

**Maximal template:** (C)(C)V(C)

**Allowed structures:**
- **V:** a, i, u
- **CV:** pa, ti, ku
- **VC:** ap, it, uk
- **CVC:** pat, tik, kup
- **CCV:** pra, tri, plu (clusters only)
- **VCC:** apt, iks (rare, primarily in VEC)
- **CCVC:** prat, trik, pluk

**Most common:** CV, CVC

---

### **Consonant Cluster Constraints**

**Word-initial clusters:**
- ✅ All 10 clusters allowed: /pɾ/, /bɾ/, /tɾ/, /dɾ/, /kɾ/, /gɾ/, /pl/, /bl/, /kl/, /gl/
- ✅ Examples: prapi, braki, trelu, dromi, krufa

**Word-medial clusters:**
- ✅ All clusters allowed between syllables
- ✅ Homorganic nasal + stop allowed: /mp/, /nt/, /ŋk/
- ✅ Examples: kampra, panti, taŋka

**Word-final clusters:**
- ⚠️ Generally avoided in basic vocabulary
- ✅ Allowed in VEC structure (V + EC without following vowel)
- ✅ Examples: agr /agɾ/, upl /upl/

---

### **Vowel Sequences**

**Adjacent vowels (hiatus):**
- ✅ Generally allowed but uncommon in basic vocabulary
- ✅ Each vowel forms separate syllable
- ✅ Examples: paela /pa.e.la/, tionu /ti.o.nu/

**Diphthongs:**
- ❌ Not phonemic (all vowels are pure)
- ⚠️ May arise naturally in fast speech
- Rule: Treat as two syllables in formal speech

## 🗣️ Pronunciation Guide

### **Special Consonants**

#### **R /ɾ/ — Alveolar Tap**
- **Not** the English /r/ (retroflex)
- **Not** the French /ʁ/ (uvular)
- **Like:** Spanish single *r*, Italian *r*, American English "butter" (the /t/ sound)
- **Articulation:** Tongue tip taps once against alveolar ridge
- **Common error:** Rolling (trilled /r/) — acceptable variant but not standard

#### **Ľ /ʎ/ — Palatal Lateral**
- **Not** regular /l/
- **Like:** Italian *gl*i, Spanish *ll* (traditional), "lli" in "million"
- **Articulation:** Lateral airflow with tongue body against palate
- **Common error:** Pronouncing as /lj/ or /li/ — acceptable approximation

#### **J̌ /dʒ/ — Voiced Postalveolar Affricate**
- Romanization: J with caron (ˇ)
- **Like:** English *j* in "judge", *g* in "gem"
- **Articulation:** Stop + fricative (d + ʒ)

#### **X /ʃ/ — Voiceless Postalveolar Fricative**
- **Like:** English *sh* in "ship", "fish"
- **Not** like /ks/ or /gz/

#### **Y /ʒ/ — Voiced Postalveolar Fricative**
- **Like:** English *s* in "pleasure", *si* in "vision"
- **Articulation:** Voiced version of X /ʃ/

---

### **Vowel Quality**

#### **A /a/ — Low Central**
- **Target:** Open, central, unrounded
- **Acceptable range:** [a] ~ [ä] ~ [ɑ]
- **Avoid:** /æ/ (English "cat"), /ʌ/ (English "cup")

#### **E /e/ — Mid Front**
- **Target:** Close-mid front, unrounded
- **Pure vowel:** No glide to /ɪ/
- **Like:** Spanish *e*, French *é*, German *e*
- **Avoid:** English "day" diphthong /eɪ/

#### **I /i/ — High Front**
- **Target:** Close front, unrounded
- **Like:** English "see", Spanish *i*, French *i*
- **Maintain length:** Don't shorten to /ɪ/ (English "sit")

#### **O /o/ — Mid Back**
- **Target:** Close-mid back, rounded
- **Pure vowel:** No glide to /ʊ/
- **Like:** Spanish *o*, German *o*
- **Avoid:** English "go" diphthong /oʊ/

#### **U /u/ — High Back**
- **Target:** Close back, rounded
- **Like:** English "boot", Spanish *u*, French *ou*
- **Maintain rounding throughout**

## 🎼 Prosody

### **Stress**

**Default pattern:** First syllable stress

**Examples:**
- **PÁpi** /ˈpa.pi/ (not pa.PÍ)
- **tríku** /ˈtɾi.ku/ (not tri.KÚ)
- **bélama** /ˈbe.la.ma/ (not be.LÁ.ma or be.la.MÁ)

**Exceptions:** None currently (may develop in future)

**Non-phonemic:** Stress does not change word meaning

---

### **Intonation**

**Statement:** Falling intonation ↘
```
ba va ti ↘
I do present
```

**Question (with interrogative):** Rising intonation ↗
```
ka be va ↗
What you do?
```

**Question (yes/no):** Rising intonation ↗
```
be va ti ↗
You do present? (Do you do it?)
```

---

### **Rhythm**

**Type:** Syllable-timed (not stress-timed)

**Characteristics:**
- Each syllable roughly equal duration
- Like: Spanish, French, Italian, Japanese
- Unlike: English, German, Dutch

**Effect:** Regular, predictable rhythm; easier for non-native speakers

## 🔄 Phonological Processes

### **VEC Pronunciation (Special Rule)**

**Context:** Vowel + EC (consonant cluster) without following vowel

**Rule:** Pronounce with **nasal final vowel** to distinguish from VECV

**Examples:**

| Written | Context | Pronunciation | Meaning |
|---------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **agr** | Isolated V+EC | /agɾã/ or /agɾ̃/ | VEC particle |
| **agra** | V+EC+V | /agɾa/ | VECV particle |
| **upl** | Isolated V+EC | /uplũ/ or /upl̃/ | VEC particle |
| **upla** | V+EC+V | /upla/ | VECV particle |

**Purpose:** Prevent confusion between VEC (isolated) and VECV (with vowel)

**Note:** Nasal quality is **non-phonemic** (doesn't create new vowels), purely prosodic marker

---

### **Consonant Assimilation**

**Generally:** Minimal assimilation (maintain clear distinctions)

**Allowed:**
- Voicing assimilation in fast speech: /pt/ → [bt]
- Nasal place assimilation: /n/ before /p,b/ → [m]

**Not required:** Learners can maintain citation forms

---

### **Elision**

**Generally:** Avoid elision in formal speech

**Casual speech:** Vowel reduction in unstressed syllables acceptable

**Not standardized:** Each speaker's natural flow

---

## 📐 Phonological Constraints

### **What Is Allowed**

✅ All CV combinations (20 consonants × 5 vowels = 100 syllables)  
✅ All VC combinations (5 vowels × 20 consonants = 100 syllables)  
✅ Consonant clusters word-initially and medially  
✅ VCV sequences (675 total)  
✅ Adjacent identical consonants (gemination): /pp/, /tt/, /kk/  
✅ Vowel hiatus (adjacent different vowels)

### **What Is Avoided**

⚠️ Consonant clusters word-finally (except VEC)  
⚠️ Triple consonant clusters  
⚠️ Syllabic consonants  
⚠️ Tone distinctions  
⚠️ Vowel length distinctions (phonemic)  
⚠️ Nasalized vowels (except VEC prosodic marker)

## 🌍 Cross-Linguistic Accessibility

### **Easy for Speakers of:**

**Romance languages** (Spanish, Portuguese, Italian, French):
- 5-vowel system familiar
- /ɾ/ tap natural
- Syllable-timed rhythm

**Germanic languages** (English, German, Dutch):
- Most consonants present
- /ʃ/, /ʒ/, /dʒ/ familiar
- Simple syllable structure

**Slavic languages** (Russian, Polish, Czech):
- Consonant clusters easy
- /ʎ/ may be familiar
- Clear vowel distinctions

**East Asian languages** (Japanese, Korean):
- Simple syllable structure
- No tones
- Clear consonant-vowel alternation

**Arabic, Hebrew:**
- Consonant inventory similar
- Stops, fricatives, nasals all present

### **Potential Challenges:**

**Japanese speakers:** /l/ vs /r/ distinction, consonant clusters  
**Arabic speakers:** /p/ vs /b/ distinction (if dialect lacks /p/)  
**English speakers:** Pure vowels (no diphthongs), /ɾ/ tap  
**Chinese speakers:** Syllable-final consonants, no tones

**Solution:** All challenges involve **common sounds** that are learnable with practice

## 🎯 Design Rationale

### **Why These 20 Consonants?**

**Coverage:** Present in majority of world languages  
**Balance:** Good distribution across manner and place  
**Distinctiveness:** Clear acoustic differences  
**Productivity:** Combine well for clusters and syllables

**Notable exclusions:**
- /θ, ð/ (English "th") — rare cross-linguistically
- /ʁ, χ/ (uvular sounds) — difficult for many
- /ɲ/ (palatal nasal) — Ľ /ʎ/ chosen instead for symmetry
- Clicks, ejectives — too rare

### **Why These 5 Vowels?**

**Universality:** Most common 5-vowel system worldwide  
**Symmetry:** Front-back, high-mid-low balanced  
**Clarity:** Maximum acoustic space between vowels  
**Productivity:** 5-way gradation creates systematic progressions

**No diphthongs:** Keeps system simple, allows pure gradation

### **Why These Clusters?**

**Natural:** Stop + liquid clusters exist in many languages  
**Productive:** 7 defined + 3 reserved = systematic expansion  
**Pronounceable:** All clusters easy for most speakers

## 🔤 Romanization

### **Standard Orthography**

**Principle:** One letter = one sound (nearly perfectly phonemic)

**Exceptions:**
- **J̌** = /dʒ/ (requires caron)
- **Ľ** = /ʎ/ (requires caron)

**Alternative romanizations** (if diacritics unavailable):
- J̌ → J or DJ
- Ľ → LJ or LY

**Consistency:** Use standard when possible, alternatives when necessary
